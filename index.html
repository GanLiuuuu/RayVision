Abstract

This project presents the development of a Remotely Operated Vehicle (ROV) with a biomimetic shell inspired by the ray species. The ROV is equipped with a neural network-based target recognition system capable of detecting and identifying underwater organisms. The system uses bounding boxes to localize and classify the captured organisms, enhancing the ROV's capabilities for underwater exploration and research. The integration of biomimicry and artificial intelligence techniques offers a novel approach to underwater robotics, enabling efficient and accurate identification of marine life forms. This abstract summarizes the design, implementation, and performance evaluation of the ROV and its target recognition system in underwater environments.
